Confused about float property??

What is float property?

Float property is used to place content side-by-side. If you want to give a column like structure using div tag, it can be done using float property i.e. using float: left or right.

Below are certain example of the same:

Code:

<div style="width:900px; margin:auto;">
<p>1)
<div style="float:left; background-color:red;">Hi!! I am Red.</div>
<div style="float:left;background-color:blue;">Hi!! I am Blue.</div>
<div style="float:left; background-color: green;">Hi!! I am Green.</div>
</br>
</p>
                       <p>2)
<div style="float:right; background-color:red;">Hi!! I am Red.</div>
<div style="float:right;background-color:blue;">Hi!! I am Blue.</div>
<div style="float:right; background-color: green;">Hi!! I am Green.</div>
</br>
</p>
<p>3)
<div style="float:left; background-color:red;">Hi!! I am Red.</div>
<div style="float:right;background-color:blue;">Hi!! I am Blue.</div>
<div style="float:left; background-color: green;">Hi!! I am Green.</div>
</p>
</div>

Output:



Clear property:
Using float property, it is very important to use clear property.Clear property makes sure that, no content is added alongside or after floated div.
Clear property takes values like both, left, right, none, inherit. An element with style clear disallows floating element on left or right or both sides of it. A DIV element with clear:both disallows floating element on it's either side, hence placing it among the floating DIVs created a line-break, or a new row insertion.

Example:
Code:

<div style="width:400px; clear:both;" >
<!--
When clear:both is not used.
-->
<div style="float:left; background-color:red; width:100px;">Hi!! I am Red.</div>
<div style="float:left;background-color:blue; width:100px;">Hi!! I am Blue.</div>
<div style="float:right; background-color: green; width:100px;">Hi!! I am Green.</div>
<div>This is not red.</div>
<div>This is not blue.</div>
<div>This is not green.</div>
<div style="height:40px;"></div>
<!--
When clear:both is used.
-->
<div style="float:left; background-color:red; width:100px;">Hi!! I am Red.</div>
<div style="float:left;background-color:blue; width:100px;">Hi!! I am Blue.</div>
<div style="float:right; background-color: green; width:100px;">Hi!! I am Green.</div>
<div style="clear:both;">This is not red.</div>
<div style="clear:both;">This is not blue.</div>
<div style="clear:both;">This is not green.</div>

</div>

Output:


As seen above "This is not red." is added in the empty space among the floated div. This is because clear property is not used.
When clear property is used, the text appears below the floated div.
